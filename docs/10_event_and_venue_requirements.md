# Event and Venue Requirements

What the Smart Pi demo needs from the event or venue for a reliable showcase. For event organizers and grant reporting.

## Demo area

- Stable area with access to **wall space** or a **portable projection surface** (screen material or whiteboard film).
- Table or surface for the Pi, cabling, and optional camera/projector mounting (tripod, table clamp, or brackets).
- See [01_bill_of_materials.md](01_bill_of_materials.md) for mounting and rigging; [02_build_guide.md](02_build_guide.md) for setup.

## Power

- **Nearby power** for the Raspberry Pi (fused 5V/5A USB-C) and the projector.
- Power strip and extension cord as needed (see BOM). Safe cable routing and labels reduce trip hazards.

## Lighting

- Interactive projection benefits from **controllable or dimmable lighting**. Shades-down or mid-brightness environments improve IR tracking.
- Config profiles (`classroom`, `dark_room`, `daylight`) in `configs/profiles/` help adapt to different rooms. For best results, recommend **projectors with 2200+ lumens** (mid-brightness) and placement/brightness guides (see [07_troubleshooting.md](07_troubleshooting.md) and build guide).

## Throw and calibration

- Enough **clear distance** for projector throw and for the camera to see the full projection surface.
- Calibration geometry depends on **stable placement**; avoid moving the projector or camera during the demo.

## Spares and support

- Access to **spare adapters/cables** (HDMI, micro-HDMI to VGA if using surplus projectors), extension cords, power strips, and basic mounting (tripod/clamp) improves reliability. See BOM contingency spares.

## Space

- A **semi-contained corner or room** minimizes interruptions during calibration and improves demo reliability.
